Can you explain the concept of 'Digital Logic Gates'?

Expert

Digital Logic Gates are electronic circuits that make logical decisions based on combinations of digital signals present at their inputs. They are the fundamental building blocks of digital systems. There are several types of logic gates, including AND, OR, NOT, NAND, NOR, XOR, and XNOR gates. Each gate performs a specific logical operation. For example, an AND gate outputs a high signal only if all its inputs are high, while an OR gate outputs a high signal if at least one of its inputs is high. The NOT gate, also known as an inverter, outputs the opposite of its input. NAND and NOR gates are combinations of AND and OR gates with a NOT gate, respectively. XOR and XNOR gates are used for more complex logical operations. Understanding these gates and their truth tables is crucial for designing and analyzing digital circuits.
